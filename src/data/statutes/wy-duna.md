Wyoming Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association Act
===

**TITLE 17 -- CORPORATIONS, PARTNERSHIPS AND ASSOCIATIONS**

**CHAPTER 22 -- UNINCORPORATED NONPROFIT ASSOCIATIONS**

*Wyoming Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association Act (DUNA)*

Enacted by HB 0078 (2024). Signed by Governor Gordon March 12, 2024.


## § 17-22-101. Short Title

This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Wyoming Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association Act."


## § 17-22-102. Definitions

As used in this act:

(i) "Decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association" or "DUNA" means an unincorporated association formed under this chapter;

(ii) "Member" means a person who, under the rules or practices of a decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association, may participate in the selection of persons authorized to manage the affairs of the association or in the development of policy of the association;

(iii) "Person" means an individual, corporation, business trust, partnership, limited liability company, foundation, government, governmental subdivision, agency or instrumentality, public corporation, or any other legal or commercial entity;

(iv) "Smart contract" means as defined in W.S. 17-31-102(a)(vii);

(v) "Blockchain" means as defined in W.S. 34-29-106(a)(i).


## § 17-22-103. Formation; Governing Principles

(a) A decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association may be formed for any lawful purpose by two (2) or more persons.

(b) A decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association may be formed and its operations governed by:

(i) A written agreement among its members;

(ii) A smart contract; or

(iii) A combination of both.

(c) A governing document or smart contract of a decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association shall govern the structure, governance, and activities of the association to the extent permitted by law.

(d) The affairs of a decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association shall be managed as provided in the governing document or smart contract and shall not be managed by a central entity or administrator unless such is provided for in the governing document or smart contract.


## § 17-22-104. Legal Entity

(a) A decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association is a legal entity separate from its members.

(b) A decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association may:

(i) Sue and be sued in its own name;

(ii) Hold and transfer real and personal property in its own name;

(iii) Make and perform contracts; and

(iv) Incur liabilities and sue and be sued as a result of such liabilities.


## § 17-22-105. Liability

(a) A member of a decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association is not liable for a debt, obligation, or other liability of the association solely by reason of being a member.

(b) A member of a decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association is not liable for a debt, obligation, or other liability based on conduct relating to the governance of the association, unless the governing document or smart contract provides otherwise.

(c) This section does not affect:

(i) The liability of a member that assumes personal liability by agreement; or

(ii) The liability of a member that is a tortfeasor.


## § 17-22-106. Governance; Voting

(a) Governance of a decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association shall be as provided in the governing document or smart contract.

(b) Unless the governing document or smart contract otherwise provides, decisions of a decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association shall be made by a majority vote of members entitled to vote.

(c) A smart contract may be used to conduct and record votes.

(d) Voting interests may be represented by tokens or digital assets.


## § 17-22-107. Property

(a) Property of a decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association is property of the association and not of its members.

(b) A member has no interest in the property of the association by reason of being a member.

(c) Property may be held by the association on-chain (via smart contract) or off-chain.


## § 17-22-108. Dissolution

(a) A decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association may be dissolved:

(i) By vote of the members as provided in the governing document or smart contract;

(ii) By court order; or

(iii) If the association has no members, by affirmative vote or written consent of the managers or equivalent officers.

(b) After dissolution, the affairs of the association shall be wound up as provided in the governing document or smart contract, or if no such provision exists, as directed by the persons winding up the association.

(c) After winding up, any remaining property shall be distributed as provided in the governing document or smart contract; if no provision exists, pursuant to applicable law.


## § 17-22-109. Registered Agent; Address

(a) A decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association that transacts business in Wyoming shall maintain:

(i) A registered agent in Wyoming; and

(ii) A registered office.

(b) A registered agent may be changed as provided under applicable Wyoming law.


## § 17-22-110. Application of Other Law

(a) To the extent that the provisions of this chapter conflict with any other provision of Wyoming law, the provisions of this chapter shall control with respect to decentralized unincorporated nonprofit associations.

(b) The Wyoming Uniform Electronic Transactions Act applies to decentralized unincorporated nonprofit associations.

(c) A decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association is not a corporation, limited liability company, or partnership within the meaning of other Wyoming statutes, except as expressly provided.


## § 17-22-111. Jurisdiction

Wyoming courts shall have jurisdiction over a decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association if the association:

(i) Has a registered agent in Wyoming;

(ii) Transacts business in Wyoming; or

(iii) Expressly consents to jurisdiction in Wyoming.
