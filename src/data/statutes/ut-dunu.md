Utah Uniform Unincorporated Nonprofit Association Act
===

**TITLE 16 -- CORPORATIONS**

**CHAPTER 11 -- UNIFORM UNINCORPORATED NONPROFIT ASSOCIATION ACT**

*Utah Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association Provisions*

Enacted as part of the Uniform Unincorporated Nonprofit Association Act, with DAO-relevant amendments added by SB 0163 (2024 General Session).

## Background: The DUNA Model

The "Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association" (DUNA) is a legal entity form designed for DAOs that do not have a profit motive and wish to operate as unincorporated nonprofit associations with:

- Legal personhood separate from members
- Limited liability for members
- Governance through smart contracts and token voting
- Flexibility without mandatory annual filings or fee structures

Wyoming pioneered the DUNA form in 2024. The a16z-backed Catena Foundation model and major protocol DAOs like Uniswap have considered the DUNA structure.

## Applicable Utah Law: Unincorporated Associations

Under existing Utah law, unincorporated nonprofit associations are governed by the Utah Uniform Unincorporated Nonprofit Association Act (Utah Code Title 16, Chapter 11). Key provisions applicable to DAO-like entities include:

### § 16-11-101. Short Title

This chapter is known as the "Utah Uniform Unincorporated Nonprofit Association Act."

### § 16-11-103. Definitions

As used in this chapter:

(1) "Member" means a person who, under the rules or practices of a nonprofit association, may participate in the selection of persons authorized to manage the affairs of the association or in the development of policy of the association.

(2) "Nonprofit association" means an unincorporated organization, other than one created by a trust, consisting of two or more members joined by mutual consent for a common, nonprofit purpose.

(3) "Person" includes an individual, corporation, business trust, estate, trust, partnership, limited liability company, association, government, governmental subdivision, or agency.

### § 16-11-104. Governing Principles

The governing principles of a nonprofit association:

(a) may be contained in a record or may be unwritten;

(b) supersede any inconsistent term of this chapter if the governing principles are not inconsistent with a nonwaivable provision of this chapter.

### § 16-11-106. Property

(a) A nonprofit association may acquire, hold, encumber, and transfer property.

(b) Property of a nonprofit association is not property of its members.

### § 16-11-107. Statement of Authority as to Real Property

A nonprofit association may execute and record a statement of authority to transfer real property.

### § 16-11-108. Liability

(a) A member of a nonprofit association is not personally liable, solely by reason of being a member, for a debt, obligation, or liability of the association.
