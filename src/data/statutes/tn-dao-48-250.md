Tenn. Code Ann. §§ 48-250-101 to -115
===

**TITLE 48 -- CORPORATIONS AND ASSOCIATIONS**

**CHAPTER 249 -- LIMITED LIABILITY COMPANIES (SUPPLEMENT)**

**CHAPTER 250 -- DECENTRALIZED ORGANIZATIONS**

*Tennessee Decentralized Organizations Act*

Enacted by SB 2854 / HB 2210 (112th General Assembly, 2022). Signed by Governor Lee. Effective January 1, 2023.

> **Note:** Tennessee Code Ann. Chapter 250 supplements the Tennessee LLC Act (Chapter 249). A decentralized organization formed under this chapter is simultaneously subject to Chapter 249 except where Chapter 250 provides otherwise.

---

## § 48-250-101. Short Title

This chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "Tennessee Decentralized Organizations Act."


## § 48-250-102. Definitions

As used in this chapter:

(1) "Blockchain" means a shared, immutable ledger that facilitates the process of recording transactions and tracking assets in a network that:

(A) uses cryptographic methods to link and secure the data;

(B) is distributed among multiple network participants; and

(C) is accessible to the public or to designated participants.

(2) "Decentralized organization" or "DAO" means an organization that:

(A) is organized pursuant to this chapter;

(B) operates through the use of smart contracts and blockchain technology; and

(C) may be governed primarily through member vote via tokens, algorithmic means, or smart contracts.

(3) "Governing smart contract" means the smart contract or contracts that govern the decentralized organization.

(4) "Member" means a person who has been admitted as a member of a decentralized organization.

(5) "Membership interest" means a member's share of the profits and losses of a decentralized organization and the right to receive distributions, which may be represented by tokens.

(6) "Operating agreement" means a written agreement, which may be embodied in a smart contract, among the members of a decentralized organization regarding the affairs of the decentralized organization and the conduct of its activities.

(7) "Smart contract" means a self-executing contract deployed on a blockchain in which the terms of the agreement between parties are written in code.

(8) "Token" means a digital unit issued on a blockchain that may represent a membership interest, governance right, or other entitlement.


## § 48-250-103. Formation

(a) One (1) or more persons may organize a decentralized organization.

(b) A decentralized organization is formed when:

(i) the articles of organization are filed with the secretary of state; and

(ii) the effective time of the articles of organization has occurred.

(c) The articles of organization shall include:

(i) the name of the organization;

(ii) a statement that the entity is a decentralized organization subject to this chapter;

(iii) the address of the registered agent and registered office in Tennessee; and

(iv) whether the organization is member-managed or smart-contract-managed.

(d) Unless the articles of organization provide otherwise, a decentralized organization shall be member-managed.


## § 48-250-104. Name

(a) The name of a decentralized organization shall:

(i) contain the words "Decentralized Organization," "Decentralized Autonomous Organization," "DAO," or "DO" or an abbreviation thereof; and

(ii) be distinguishable from any other entity name registered with the secretary of state.


## § 48-250-105. Powers

A decentralized organization has the same powers as a limited liability company under the Tennessee Limited Liability Company Act (Title 48, Chapter 249), including the power to:

(a) own property;

(b) contract;

(c) sue and be sued;

(d) hold digital assets and tokens on-chain;

(e) adopt, amend, and repeal an operating agreement; and

(f) engage in any lawful activity.


## § 48-250-106. Members

(a) Membership interests in a decentralized organization may be represented by tokens.

(b) Transfer of a token representing a membership interest transfers the associated rights unless the operating agreement provides otherwise.

(c) Membership interests may be issued in one or more classes, each with distinct voting, economic, or other rights.

(d) A person may become a member:

(i) as set forth in the articles of organization or operating agreement; or

(ii) by acquiring a token representing a membership interest, unless the operating agreement restricts transferability.


## § 48-250-107. Management; Smart Contract Management

(a) A decentralized organization may be:

(i) member-managed, in which case the members hold governance rights as specified in the operating agreement or articles of organization; or

(ii) smart-contract-managed, in which case the governing smart contract controls the organization's operations and governance.

(b) If a decentralized organization is smart-contract-managed, the governing smart contract shall be publicly accessible on a blockchain.

(c) Amendments to the governing smart contract may be made as specified in the operating agreement or through the governance mechanism in the smart contract itself.


## § 48-250-108. Operating Agreement

(a) A decentralized organization may have an operating agreement.

(b) The operating agreement may be:

(i) written;

(ii) embodied in a smart contract; or

(iii) a combination of both.

(c) The operating agreement may:

(i) restrict transfers of membership interests;

(ii) establish governance procedures;

(iii) allocate distributions;

(iv) limit the duties of members; and

(v) provide for amendment of the organization's governing smart contract.

(d) To the extent of any conflict between the operating agreement and the articles of organization, the articles of organization govern.


## § 48-250-109. Standards of Conduct; No Implied Fiduciary Duties

(a) A member of a decentralized organization does not owe a fiduciary duty to the organization or to other members solely by reason of being a member, unless the operating agreement expressly provides otherwise.

(b) A member shall act in a manner consistent with:

(i) the operating agreement; and

(ii) the governing smart contract.

(c) This section shall not limit the liability of a member for:

(i) fraud, bad faith, willful misconduct, or knowing violation of law; or

(ii) conduct prohibited by the operating agreement.

(d) The operating agreement may establish standards of conduct for members, including fiduciary duties.


## § 48-250-110. Distributions

(a) Distributions to members shall be made as provided in the operating agreement.

(b) A decentralized organization shall not make a distribution if, after giving effect to the distribution, the total liabilities of the organization exceed the fair value of its total assets.

(c) A distribution may be made in the form of digital assets, tokens, or other property.


## § 48-250-111. Notice of Restrictions on Transfer or Duties

A decentralized organization that restricts the transfer of membership interests or limits the duties of members shall:

(a) include a statement of such restrictions in the articles of organization; and

(b) ensure that such restrictions are reasonably accessible to members, including through the operating agreement or governing smart contract.


## § 48-250-112. Events of Dissolution; Dissociation

(a) A member may dissociate from a decentralized organization as provided in the operating agreement or governing smart contract.

(b) Unless the operating agreement provides otherwise, a member may dissociate by:

(i) transferring all of the member's membership interests; or

(ii) receiving the consent of a majority of the other members.


## § 48-250-113. Dissolution

(a) A decentralized organization shall be dissolved:

(i) upon approval of a majority of the members, unless the operating agreement requires a greater percentage;

(ii) by vote of members as specified in the operating agreement or governing smart contract;

(iii) upon the occurrence of an event specified in the articles of organization;

(iv) by a decree of judicial dissolution; or

(v) by administrative dissolution.

(b) A decentralized organization shall wind up its affairs after dissolution.

(c) The organizers or members responsible for winding up shall file a statement of dissolution with the secretary of state.


## § 48-250-114. Conversions; Mergers

(a) A decentralized organization may convert to another form of business entity pursuant to Title 48.

(b) A decentralized organization may merge with another entity pursuant to Title 48.

(c) A foreign business entity may convert to a Tennessee decentralized organization if it satisfies the requirements of this chapter.


## § 48-250-115. Application of Other Law

(a) To the extent the provisions of this chapter are inconsistent with the Tennessee Limited Liability Company Act (Title 48, Chapter 249), the provisions of this chapter control.

(b) A decentralized organization is subject to all applicable state and federal laws, including securities laws, money transmission laws, and tax laws.

(c) The Tennessee Uniform Electronic Transactions Act applies to decentralized organizations.
