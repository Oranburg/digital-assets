## 3. Terms with Factual Errors or Outdated Information

### Definite Errors

1. **USDT (Tether)** -- "generates substantial profits (approximately $1 billion)" is significantly outdated. Tether reported approximately $6.2 billion in net profit for 2023 and over $13 billion for 2024. The "$1 billion" figure may come from quarterly figures or pre-2023 data. Should be corrected or removed.

2. **Solana** -- "Founded in 2020" is incorrect. Solana Labs was founded in 2017 by Anatoly Yakovenko. Mainnet beta launched in March 2020. The glossary conflates launch date with founding date.

3. **Hedera** -- "founded in 2018-2019 by two U.S. Air Force cryptography specialists" is misleading. Leemon Baird is a computer scientist (PhD from Carnegie Mellon) who previously worked at various companies. Mance Harmon was formerly at the Air Force Academy as a course director, but neither is accurately described as an "Air Force cryptography specialist." The company was incorporated in 2018.

4. **Genius Act** -- Described as "U.S. federal legislation signed into law." The existing term in the digital-assets project (genius-act.json) treats it as enacted (Pub. L. No. 119-27). The glossary definition is consistent with that but the description of three categories ("federally qualified," "state-qualified," "state-issued") should be verified against the final enrolled text; some of these category names may be glossary shorthand rather than statutory language. Also: "The Genius Act completely excludes algorithmic stablecoins as a permitted option" -- the Act does not use the word "algorithmic"; it requires reserve backing, which functionally excludes algorithmic models, but the phrasing is imprecise.

5. **CBDC** -- "Wyoming has explicitly prohibited CBDC implementation within the state (passed 2025)" needs verification. Wyoming's anti-CBDC legislation (SF 0090) was introduced in the 2023 session. The 2025 date may be incorrect.

### Potentially Outdated Claims

6. **Fiat-backed Stablecoin** -- "$150 billion" in Treasury holdings and "17th largest holder" will shift with market growth. These should be date-stamped or given as approximate ranges.

7. **Stablecoin** -- "$200-300 billion" market cap was approximately correct in late 2025 but the market was trending above $230B by early 2026. This range may already be stale.

8. **Clarity Act** -- "approximately 78 Democrats had joined Republicans" is a snapshot claim that will not age well. Should either cite a date or be removed.

9. **USDC** -- "expected to continue growing market share and potentially surpass USDT by 2030 according to industry projections" is speculative and unattributed.

### Duplicate Entry

10. **Homomorphic Encryption** appears twice: once as "Homomorphic Encryption/Cryptography" (Blockchain section, line 116) and once as "Homomorphic Encryption" (Data & Cryptography section, line 345). The definitions are nearly identical. Should be consolidated.

11. **Reserve Requirement** appears twice: once in the Stablecoins section (line 40) and once in the Regulatory section (line 248). The second version adds discussion of "Treasury-backed vs. deposit-backed vs. algorithmic" reserve types. Should be consolidated.


## 4. Missing L1 Fundamentals

The following terms from the requested list are NOT in the glossary and should be added:

**Already in glossary (no action needed):**
- 51% attack -- NOT in glossary (but exists in digital-assets project)
- BFT 1/3 liveness -- NOT in glossary (but exists in digital-assets project as aBFT entry covers part of this)
- Proof-of-work -- IN glossary
- Proof-of-stake -- IN glossary
- Sidechain -- IN glossary
- Zero-knowledge proof -- IN glossary

**Missing and should be added:**
- **51% Attack** -- Not defined as a standalone term despite being a fundamental security concept.
- **Finality** -- Deterministic finality is defined, but general finality (the property that a confirmed transaction cannot be reversed) is not defined as its own term.
- **Fork** (hard fork / soft fork) -- Not in glossary. Fundamental concept for understanding protocol upgrades and chain splits.
- **Hash Function** -- Not in glossary. Foundational cryptographic primitive underlying all blockchain security.
- **Merkle Tree** -- Not in glossary. Key data structure for efficient transaction verification.
- **Nonce** -- Not in glossary. Core concept in proof-of-work mining and transaction ordering.
- **Gas Fees** -- Not in glossary. The cost mechanism for computation on Ethereum and EVM chains. "Surge Pricing" covers the concept obliquely but gas fees as a general term are missing.
- **Block Time** -- Not in glossary. Determines throughput and user experience.
- **Mempool** -- Mentioned in the front-running definition but not defined as its own term.
- **Node** -- Used throughout but never defined.
- **Validator** -- Used throughout but never defined as its own term.
- **Sharding** -- Not in glossary. Key scalability technique.
- **Rollup** (optimistic and zero-knowledge) -- Mentioned in Layer 2 definition but not defined separately. These are the dominant L2 scaling approach.
- **zkSNARK** -- Not in glossary.
- **zkSTARK** -- Not in glossary.
- **State Channel** -- Mentioned in Layer 2 definition but not defined separately.
- **Liveness Attack (1/3 threshold)** -- Not a standalone entry; aBFT entry covers some of this ground but the specific attack vector is absent.
